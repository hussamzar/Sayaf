Choosing the Right Bat7a for Your Project: Your Comprehensive Guide to Sand Types

Bat7a is one of the most essential materials in any construction project, and choosing the right type plays a crucial role in the quality and durability of the execution. Did you know that there are different types of bat7a, each with its specific uses? In this article, we will review the differences between the most famous types of bat7a available on the market, such as Riyadh bat7a and Taima bat7a, and highlight the characteristics of each and how to choose the most suitable one for your project, whether for plastering, backfilling, or aesthetic finishes.

Riyadh Bat7a: Best for Interior Plastering and Professional Finishes

Riyadh bat7a is known for its high purity and relatively fine grains, making it the perfect choice for interior plastering. Its fineness helps the worker achieve a professional and smooth finish on the walls, ensuring a perfectly level and smooth surface ready for paint or wallpaper. It also provides good flexibility during application, which reduces the chances of cracks appearing. Riyadh bat7a is essential for achieving a professional finish and high quality in interior finishing work.

Taima Bat7a: For Strength and Distinctive Exterior Decorations

In contrast, Taima bat7a is coarser and stronger than Riyadh bat7a, thanks to the nature of its grains which gives it greater load-bearing capacity. These characteristics make it ideal for use on exterior building facades, especially when aiming for distinctive decorations or patterns. The coarseness of Taima bat7a grains provides excellent cohesion with cement, giving the facade extra strength. It is usually used with white cement to highlight colors, and its price is relatively higher than Riyadh bat7a due to its unique properties and high quality in resisting erosion factors.

Secrets of Professional Plastering: Expert Tips

Good plastering is the foundation for professionally and durably finishing any walls or ceilings. But what are the secrets behind plastering that lasts a long time and looks flawless? In this comprehensive guide, our experts share the best tips and practices to ensure the quality of your plastering work. We will review the importance of choosing the right bat7a, the correct ratios for mixing materials, and how to avoid common problems like cracks and peeling. Follow these guidelines to achieve the best results in your projects.

The Importance of Good Surface Preparation

Before starting the plastering process, you must ensure that the surface is completely clean and free of dust, oils, or any impurities that could affect the mortar's adhesion. Any large cracks or holes must also be treated and filled to ensure a completely level surface.

Correct Ratios for Mixing Mortar

The success of plastering largely depends on the correct ratios of sand, cement, and water. Using the wrong ratio can lead to weak plaster or cracks appearing later. It is preferable to use fine and clean plastering sand, mixed with cement in specific ratios recommended by experts, with the addition of a suitable amount of water to get a consistent and easy-to-apply texture.

Professional Application Techniques

  • Tartasha First: A thin layer of tartasha must be applied before starting the plastering, to increase the surface roughness and improve mortar adhesion.
  • Plastering Layers: It is preferable to apply plaster in two or three layers instead of one thick layer, to ensure consistency and reduce the chances of cracking. Each layer should be left to dry partially before applying the next.
  • Watering After Plastering: Spraying the surface with water regularly for a few days after plastering helps with the curing process and prevents cracks caused by rapid drying.

Tartasha: The Essential Step for Wall Strength Before Plastering

Before starting the plastering and final finishing work on walls, there is a vital, indispensable step: tartasha. This initial layer is not just a formality; it is the foundation that ensures the plaster adheres effectively and increases wall strength and resistance to external factors. In this article, we explain how to use tartasha correctly, and what its importance is in protecting concrete structures. At Desert Sand Est., we provide you with the best and highest quality tartasha in terms of roughness and cleanliness, to ensure long-lasting results.

What is Tartasha and Why is It Important?

Tartasha is a rough, initial layer of cement mortar that is sprayed onto concrete or brick surfaces before applying the main plaster layers. Its primary function is to create a rough (textured) surface that allows subsequent plaster layers to adhere more strongly, which prevents them from peeling or falling off over time. It also provides a primary protective layer for the concrete and helps to insulate it.

How Does Tartasha Contribute to Wall Strength?

Thanks to the tartasha layer, the surface becomes more cohesive and consistent. This cohesion reduces the chances of cracks occurring in subsequent plaster layers and increases the walls' resistance to impacts and moisture. Good tartasha means stronger and more durable plastering, and therefore, stronger and more resilient walls.

Specifications of High-Quality Tartasha

  • Sufficient Roughness: The tartasha must be rough enough to provide a good surface for adhesion, without being excessively rough to cause problems with plastering.
  • Cleanliness: Using clean, washed sand free of salts and impurities ensures the strength of the tartasha and prevents spots or problems from appearing later.
  • Correct Mix: The ratio of cement, sand, and water must be balanced to obtain a strong and cohesive mortar.

Concrete: The Foundation of Strong Construction Projects and Its Multiple Uses

Concrete is the backbone of any modern construction project due to its exceptional strength and durability. Its uses are not limited to creating slabs and foundations, but extend to a wide range of applications in concrete structures, from columns and walls to ceilings and bridges. Types of Concrete and Their Uses

  • Ordinary Concrete: Used in foundations, footings, and floors that are not subjected to high tensile stresses.
  • Reinforced Concrete: This is the most common type, where concrete is reinforced with steel rebar to increase its tensile strength. It is used in columns, bridges, slabs, and main foundations.
  • Prestressed Concrete: The rebar is stressed before or after the concrete is poured, which gives it extra strength and is used in structures that require wide spans or heavy loads.
  • Lightweight and Heavyweight Concrete: Its density varies depending on the added materials and is used for special applications such as insulation or radiation shielding.

Concrete slabs are one of the most important stages of any project, whether it is a floor slab, a roof slab, or a foundation slab. The quality of these slabs depends on the quality of the concrete used, its correct mixing, pouring, and curing. Speez (Backfill): Its Vital Role in Stabilizing Foundations and Road Backfilling

In modern construction and infrastructure development, backfill materials play a role no less important than primary construction materials. Speez, or backfill material, is the cornerstone in achieving stability and durability. This article highlights its vital uses, whether in backfilling works for buildings to stabilize foundations, or in road backfilling projects to ensure their levelness and load-bearing capacity.

What is Speez?

Speez is a mixture of gravel, sand, and soil that is sorted and processed to have specific characteristics in terms of grain size and hardness. It is primarily used as a base layer or backfill layer in various construction projects.

Uses of Speez in Construction and Roads

  • Foundation Stabilization: Speez is used to create a stable base layer under building footings and foundations, which helps to distribute loads evenly and prevent building settlement.
  • Site Backfilling: It is used to raise the level of floors, fill gaps, and level sites before starting construction.
  • Road Backfilling: Speez is an essential component in the base and subgrade layers when building roads, as it provides a strong and stable layer that can withstand the pressure of traffic.
  • Landscaping: It can be used in landscaping and garden work to create different levels or to backfill specific areas.
